A Software System to Protect SME Assets & Resources in the AI Era
Context
Small and medium enterprises (SMEs) are increasingly exposed to AI-driven risks: automated spear-phishing, AI-generated disinformation targeting brand reputation, adversarial use of internal data leaked to public LLMs, shadow AI tool adoption by employees, and supply-chain compromise via AI-powered attacks. Unlike enterprises, SMEs lack dedicated security teams and large budgets.
Your Task
Design a unified protection platform for SMEs (10–500 employees) covering their most critical assets: data, people, intellectual property, financial accounts, and operational continuity. The system must be economically viable and operable without a full-time security team.
Key Requirements to Address
-	Asset inventory and classification: data, devices, accounts, third-party integrations
-	AI-specific threat surface: prompt injection, LLM data leakage, deepfake fraud (voice/video)
-	Access governance: least-privilege enforcement, offboarding automation, shadow IT detection
-	Continuous compliance posture against relevant standards (ISO 27001, GDPR, SOC 2 lite)
-	Incident playbooks executable by non-security staff
-	Cost model: tiered, pay-as-you-grow pricing aligned with SME constraints
-	Integration with common SME tools: Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Slack, QuickBooks, etc.
Deliverables
1.	System Architecture Diagram — Logical and deployment view, showing integration touchpoints with third-party SaaS tools and on-premise assets.
2.	Design Document (max 600 words) — Core architectural decisions: build vs. buy, multi-tenancy model, AI-threat detection strategy, and data privacy guarantees for customer data.
3.	Team & Delivery Plan — How would you staff and sequence delivery of a v1 over 6 months? What is the riskiest assumption to validate first?
4.	AI Governance Module — Detail your approach to detecting and governing employee use of external AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Copilot) and the risks this introduces.
